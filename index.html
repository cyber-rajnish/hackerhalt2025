Learning Outcomes

By the end of this program, participants will be able to:

  • Understand AI technologies, lifecycle, and security implications.
  • Identify, assess, and prioritize strategic and operational AI risks.
  • Apply NIST AI RMF and NIST CSF to governance and control implementation.
  • Navigate global AI regulations: EU AI Act, US EO 14110, OMB M-24-10, and more.
  • Build governance structures, compliance roadmaps, and AI incident response capabilities.
  • Integrate strategic board-level decisions into operational execution.

Program Agenda

Day 1 — Strategy Meets Governance

Goal: Build a shared foundation for strategic oversight and set governance direction.

Session 1 — Strategic Understanding of AI

AI fundamentals, business impact, lifecycle, and governance touchpoints.

Session 2 — The AI Risk Landscape

Enterprise-level risks, risk appetite, and mapping to NIST AI RMF & CSF.

Session 3 — Designing AI Governance Structures

NIST frameworks, committees, ethics boards, and escalation channels.

Session 4 — Regulatory Compliance at the Strategic Level

Deep dive into EU AI Act, US EO 14110, OMB M-24-10, and state laws.

Session 5 — Executive Decision Simulation

Scenario-based exercise on a high-risk AI launch in a regulated sector.

Day 2 — Operational Execution & Response

Goal: Equip teams to execute governance, implement controls, and respond to incidents.

Session 1 — AI Foundations for Practitioners

AI system components, attack surfaces, and lifecycle threat mapping.

Session 2 — AI Security Risk Assessment

Technical risks (prompt injection, model theft) and operational risks.

Session 3 — Implementing AI Governance Controls

Translating NIST AI RMF MANAGE into controls and applying CSF.

Session 4 — Compliance in Practice

Operational requirements for EU AI Act, US EO 14110, and state laws.

Session 5 — Incident Response for AI Security

Incident classification, escalation, and post-incident governance updates.

Program Integration

Day 1 → Day 2 Link

Strategic decisions become operational tasks. Governance charts, risk matrices, and compliance maps from Day 1 are used as inputs for Day 2 exercises.

Case Study Continuity

The same high-risk AI project runs through both days — from board-level approval to operational deployment and incident handling.
